# Introduction {.unnumbered}

Something is broken. You can feel it.

You vote, but your choices are pre-selected by a system you didn't design. You watch debates that aren't debates. You read platforms that aren't platforms. Every four years, you're handed two options and told this is freedom.

It's not.

The American experiment was revolutionary — a government of the people, by the people, for the people. But somewhere along the way, the machinery of democracy was captured. Not by a tyrant. Not by a foreign power. By the slow, grinding logic of a system that rewards polarization, punishes compromise, and treats citizens as spectators in their own republic.

This book is about the machine. How it works. Why it's failing. And how we fix it.

::: callout
**This is not a partisan book.** The structural failures of American democracy hurt voters on the left, the right, and everywhere in between. The reforms in these pages have been championed by conservatives, progressives, and independents alike. If you came here looking for someone to blame, you'll find plenty — but the villains are incentive structures, not people.
:::

## What You'll Find Here

**Part I: The Problem** takes apart the current system piece by piece. We'll look at how first-past-the-post voting creates a two-party duopoly, how gerrymandering lets politicians choose their voters instead of the other way around, and how the campaign finance system turns representation into a commodity.

**Part II: The Solutions** presents proven, practical reforms that are already working — in American cities, in American states, and in democracies around the world. Ranked choice voting. Open primaries. Independent redistricting commissions. Democracy dollars. These aren't utopian fantasies. They're tested systems with real track records.

**Part III: Making It Happen** is the playbook. How citizens — not politicians — are driving these reforms through ballot initiatives, grassroots organizing, and sheer stubbornness. And what you can do, starting today.

::: deep-dive
### How to Read This Book

You don't have to read it in order. Each chapter stands alone. If you already understand the problem and want solutions, skip to Part II. If you want to get involved right now, jump to Chapter 12: The Citizen's Toolkit.

But if you have the time, start from the beginning. The solutions make more sense when you understand the disease.
:::
